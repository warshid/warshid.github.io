Saltire Scholar

This summer I will be on placement with AIG, one of the world's biggest companies. This internship comes courtasy of the Saltire Foundation.

Our team created a product called 'eyeSore' which aimed to empower local communities to act on problem issues and come together.

Find the local eyeSores around you. By using this app individuals can report problems within their local area by sending visual and gps data to the eyeSore server. eyeSore brings together the local community, authorities and businesses to tackle local problems.

We attended an awards ceremony held by Prime Minister David Cameron at 10 Downing Street and were invited to present our product at the 'Silicon Valley comes to Tech City' conference in London. While also being featured in BBC News coverage.

The Hackin' The City Hackathon brought together 55 students from a dozen of top universities in the UK to pit their skills in a round-the-clock hackathon to design and develop an application over 24 hours.

The aim of our project was to enable the creation and validation of safe buildings that have optimal escape routes in the event of disasters. Through the use of pathfinding algorithms, building floor plans can be analysed and statistics presented. Subsequently through these adjustments, improvements can be made in order to aid safety in the event of an emergency.

On Sunday morning, 12 teams presented their value proposition, technology solution and ran a live demo of their projects. Judges after long deliberation came up with six finalists. By 1530 on Sunday after 25 minutes of intense debate, senior judges awarded our team 3rd place and we each received Amazon vouchers.
